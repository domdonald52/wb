P-Chart vs Flight Manual + AC91-3 — Method Comparison

Both methods are CASO-4-compliant but use different correction factors. This page shows where they disagree. Discrepancy = (FM+AC91-3 − P-chart) / P-chart × 100%. Positive % = FM+AC91-3 gives the longer (more conservative) distance. Negative % = P-chart gives the longer distance.

Notes: PA-38 uses linear-coefficient FM data (base distance + per-1000ft PA + per-10°C OAT corrections) rather than a tabular FM; the same engine path applies AC91-3 corrections on top. Negative diffs mean the P-chart is more conservative; positive diffs mean the FM+AC91-3 layer is. Both methods are CASO-4-compliant; differences reflect different paths to the same regulatory standard.
